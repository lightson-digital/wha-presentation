# Prompt for Claude.ai — WHA Presentation Artifact

Use /lights-on-brand-guidelines, /lights-on-brand-voice, and /frontend-design to build this as an interactive HTML presentation artifact.

## What to build

A fullscreen presentation deck I can screen-share on Zoom. 8 main slides + 5 backup slides. Arrow keys and click to navigate. Speaker notes visible only to me (toggle with 'N' key). Current slide number shown.

## Design constraints

- Apply Lights On Digital brand colors and typography from /lights-on-brand-guidelines
- Dark background, light text — optimized for screen share on Zoom
- CRITICAL: Slides must be minimal text. Follow the "On slide" notes below for what goes ON the slide. Everything under "Spoken" is speaker notes only — never on the slide.
- Large type (40pt+ equivalent for titles, 24pt+ for body). Max 5-6 elements per slide.
- No bullet points on slides — use spatial layout, icons, or visual hierarchy instead
- The 5-stage Guest Journey framework (Discover → Book → Arrival → Stay → Reconnect) should be a consistent visual element that appears on Slides 3, 4, and 5 with different overlays

## Interaction

- Left/Right arrow keys to navigate
- 'N' key toggles speaker notes panel (hidden by default — only I can see it)
- Progress indicator (slide X of Y)
- Smooth transitions between slides

## SLIDE 3: Guest Journey Framework

**On slide:**
- Title: "Every hotel has money hiding in these five stages."
- Horizontal 5-stage flow with icons — stage names and one-line questions ONLY:
  - Discover — "How do guests find you?"
  - Book — "When they're ready, do they convert?"
  - Arrival — "What happens before check-in?"
  - Stay — "Does the experience create advocates?"
  - Reconnect — "Do past guests come back?"
- NO sub-bullets on the slide. The detail is all spoken.

**Speaker notes:**
- "A 20-room motel and a 200-room resort have the same five stages — the gaps just show up in different places."
- "When a member tells you 'we're not getting enough bookings,' they're stuck in Discover. 'Full on Expedia but website gets nothing' — that's Book. 'Great reviews but nobody comes back' — that's Reconnect."
- ASK: "Which of these five stages do you hear your members complain about most?" (Pause 7 seconds)
- Discover details: Search engines, Google Hotel Ads, OTA listings, AI chatbots (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini), social media, paid ads
- Book details: Rate integrity, website conversion, OTA vs. direct mix, optimized rates
- Arrival details: Pre-arrival comms, preference capture, upsells
- Stay details: Email capture, upsells, review capture, operational feedback
- Reconnect details: Email marketing, repeat visits, review management

## SLIDE 5: Olympic Lodge Walkthrough

**On slide:**
- Title: "Olympic Lodge by Ayres, Port Angeles"
- Same 5-stage framework with red/green scoring overlaid:
  - Discover: 2/5 (red) — "Invisible"
  - Book: 2/5 (red) — "14 OTAs, no direct"
  - Arrival: — (gray) — "Not assessed"
  - Stay: — (gray) — "Not assessed"
  - Reconnect: 2/5 (red) — "1,100 reviews, 0 responses"
- Keep it visual — the scorecard IS the slide. Don't add finding bullets.

**Speaker notes:**
- Discover: Instagram 1-2x/week (benchmark 3-5x), all nature photos, 944 followers, no paid ads. Invisible.
- Book: Google "Olympic Lodge Port Angeles" — 14 OTA channels bidding. Their own website? Not there. Every booking pays 15-25% commission that could be $0.
- Rate parity: OTAs at $183, 52% premium over Red Lion ($120), justified by 4.5 stars. Can't capitalize if guests can't book direct.
- Reconnect: 4.5 stars, 1,146 Google reviews. Zero responses.
- "None of these require a renovation. Google Hotel Ads — free. Review responses — free. Instagram — free. Tuesday-afternoon fixes."
- "This is what 5% looks like. Not one big thing. Ten small things nobody's doing."
- ASK: "Anything here surprise you? Or does this feel familiar?"
